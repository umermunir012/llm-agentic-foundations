ASRS Report: ACN 1234567
Date: March 2023
Report Type: Air Carrier Captain Report

Narrative:
During descent into a busy Class B airport, ATC issued a series of rapid-fire
amendments to our arrival routing. We were given three heading changes and two altitude
changes within a span of about two minutes. During this high-workload period, we
inadvertently descended through our assigned altitude by approximately 300 feet before
the TCAS issued a Resolution Advisory (RA). We immediately followed the RA guidance and
climbed back to our assigned altitude.

Contributing Factors:
- High ATC workload leading to rapid instruction changes
- Cockpit task saturation during descent phase
- Fatigue: both pilots had been on duty for approximately 11 hours
- Unfamiliarity with the specific arrival procedure (recently modified STAR)

Corrective Actions Taken:
The crew filed this ASRS report. The airline reviewed the event and determined that
additional CRM (Crew Resource Management) emphasis on task prioritization during
high-workload phases would be addressed in recurrent training. The captain recommended
that the airline update its approach briefing template to include a step for verifying
familiarity with recently modified procedures.

Lessons Learned:
This event highlights the importance of maintaining altitude awareness even during
periods of high ATC communication workload. The TCAS system functioned as designed
and served as the last line of defense. Proactive altitude callouts by the pilot
monitoring could have caught the deviation earlier.
