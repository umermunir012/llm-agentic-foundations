NTSB Report: AAR-06-03
Date: August 27, 2006
Location: Lexington, Kentucky
Aircraft: Comair Flight 5191, Bombardier CRJ-100ER

Summary:
On August 27, 2006, about 0607 eastern daylight time, Comair flight 5191, a
Bombardier CL-600-2B19, N431CA, crashed during takeoff from Blue Grass Airport in
Lexington, Kentucky. The flight crew mistakenly attempted to take off from runway 26,
which was too short for the aircraft, instead of the assigned runway 22. The aircraft
overran the end of the runway, struck a berm, went through an airport perimeter fence,
and crashed into trees. Of the 50 persons on board, 49 were killed. The first officer
survived with serious injuries.

Probable Cause:
The NTSB determined that the probable cause was the flight crew's failure to use
available cues and aids to identify the correct runway for takeoff. Contributing to
the accident was the tower controller's failure to maintain awareness of the airplane's
position on the airport surface, the FAA's failure to require that all runway
crossings be approved by specific ATC clearance, and the airport authority's failure
to maintain adequate lighting on the taxiway leading to the departure runway.

Safety Recommendations:
The NTSB recommended cockpit procedures to positively confirm the correct runway
before takeoff, including verification of the heading indicator against the assigned
runway. Additional recommendations addressed airport surface detection equipment,
improved runway signage, and enhanced ATC procedures for monitoring aircraft on the
airport surface.
