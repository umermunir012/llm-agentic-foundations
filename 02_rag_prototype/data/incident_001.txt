NTSB Report: AAR-10-01
Date: February 12, 2009
Location: Clarence Center, New York
Aircraft: Colgan Air Flight 3407, Bombardier DHC-8-402

Summary:
On February 12, 2009, about 2217 eastern standard time, Colgan Air flight 3407, a
Bombardier DHC-8-402, N200WQ, crashed into a residence in Clarence Center, New York,
during an instrument landing system (ILS) approach to runway 23 at Buffalo-Niagara
International Airport. The captain, first officer, two flight attendants, and 45
passengers were killed. One person on the ground was also killed, and the occupied
residence was destroyed.

Probable Cause:
The National Transportation Safety Board determines that the probable cause of this
accident was the captain's inappropriate response to the activation of the stick shaker,
which led to an aerodynamic stall from which the airplane did not recover. Contributing
to the accident were: (1) the flight crew's failure to monitor airspeed in relation to
the rising position of the low-speed cue, (2) the flight crew's failure to adhere to
sterile cockpit procedures, (3) the captain's failure to effectively manage the flight,
and (4) Colgan Air's inadequate procedures for airspeed selection and monitoring during
approaches in icing conditions.

Safety Recommendations:
The NTSB issued numerous safety recommendations following this accident including
requirements for pilot fatigue management, enhanced training standards for stall
recovery, and minimum 1,500 hours total flight time for first officers at Part 121
carriers. These recommendations led to the Airline Safety and Federal Aviation
Administration Extension Act of 2010.
