FAA Advisory Circular: AC 150/5200-32B
Subject: Reporting Wildlife Aircraft Strikes

Purpose:
This advisory circular provides guidance on reporting wildlife strikes to the FAA.
Wildlife strikes pose a significant safety hazard to aircraft operations. The FAA
National Wildlife Strike Database contains records dating back to 1990 and receives
approximately 17,000 strike reports annually.

Reporting Requirements:
Pilots, airport operators, engine manufacturers, and aircraft maintenance personnel
are encouraged to report all wildlife strikes using FAA Form 5200-7 (Bird/Other
Wildlife Strike Report). Reports should be filed within 24 hours of the event through
the FAA Wildlife Strike Database online portal.

Key Statistics:
- Over 300,000 wildlife strikes have been reported to the FAA since 1990
- Bird strikes account for approximately 97% of reported wildlife strikes
- Deer and coyotes are the most commonly struck mammals on airport surfaces
- The estimated annual cost of wildlife strikes to US civil aviation exceeds $900 million
- Engines are the aircraft component most frequently damaged by bird strikes
- The US Airways Flight 1549 "Miracle on the Hudson" in 2009 was caused by a dual
  engine ingestion of Canada geese at approximately 2,800 feet AGL

Mitigation Strategies:
Airports are required to conduct wildlife hazard assessments (WHA) under 14 CFR Part
139. Common mitigation strategies include habitat modification, wildlife harassment
and dispersal programs, and coordination with local wildlife management agencies.
The FAA recommends that airports within the approach and departure corridors maintain
environments that do not attract hazardous wildlife.
